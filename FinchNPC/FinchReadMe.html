Introduction

When the Iron Tutu competition rolled around in February of 2005, I knew that I lacked the familiarity with BG1 that would hold up against the time constraints of the event, but I had the will to do something Tutuesque to celebrate the theme of the weekend. That weekend turned into a month, resulting in this little endeavor, and is still undergoing various tweaks to improve and fill out the character.

This mod includes:

  • One neutral good, gnome cleric of Deneir
  • One NPC-related quest
  • One new soundset of dubious entertainment value
  • 2-4 banters with each of the Bioware NPCs
  • Dozens and dozens of interjections
  • Banter with Indira and Mur'Neth custom NPCs, if installed
  • Some PC-init talks

Finch is first found in Candlekeep ([1571.1942]), where she has been studying recently. The player can choose to talk to her during the prologue, or wait. You can encounter her again later down the road, regardless of whether you talk to her in Candlekeep.

To Play Finch NPC - Instructions for RAR install:

  1. Extract the contents of FinchNPCv3.rar into the Tutu directory on your computer. (WinRAR is recommended. WinAce is known for poorly extracting mod files, causing woe to the poor players using it.)
  2. Double-click Setup-FinchNPC.exe. A console window (DOS window) opens up and asks you to choose a language option. 0 = English, 1 = French, 2 = Spanish, 3 = German. Type your number choice and enter.
  3. You will be prompted whether or not you wish to install Finch. Answer Yes.
  4. You'll see a list of files being copied, and scripts and dialogue being compiled. This process may take a minute or two on a slower system.
  5. After the text window tells you to press enter to exit, there is one final stage--decompressing the compressed audio included with Finch to the standard WAV format. The window will fill with text: THIS IS NORMAL. This process may take a few minutes on a slower system.
  6. Start a new game and enjoy! (You should be able to use a game in progress, too.)

If anything goes wrong with the process, or you later wish to uninstall Finch, run the Setup-FinchNPC.exe program once more and select the U option.

To Play Finch NPC - Instructions for Auto-Extracting Install:

  1. Double-click FinchNPCv3.exe.
  2. You will be asked whether or not you wish to install Finch. Answer Yes.
  3. You will be prompted to confirm the directory where you have Tutu installed. If the directory provided is incorrect, you will have to browse to the correct location for a successful install.
  4. A console window (DOS window) opens up and asks you to choose a language option. 0 = English, 1 = French, 2 = Spanish, 3 = German. Type your number choice and enter.
  5. You will be prompted whether or not you wish to install Finch again. Enter Y for Yes.
  6. You'll see a list of files being copied, scripts and dialogue being compiled, and sounds being extracted. This process may take a minute or two on a slower system.
  7. Start a new game and enjoy! (You should be able to use a game in progress, too.)

If you later wish to uninstall Finch, run the Setup-FinchNPC.exe program and select the U option.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I have to start a new game?

A: No. There is one prologue encounter that may be missed if you use an existing game, but Finch and her quest are available without starting a new game. You may also notice a few weird things if you use a savegame from a pre-v2 Finch install after you've updated to v2 or later.

Q: Help! Finch talked to me in Candlekeep, then left!

A: This is perfectly normal. Finch is not party-joinable until you meet up with her again in Beregost.

Q: Help! I want Finch in my party! Where do I find her in Beregost?

A: In the Red Sheaf Inn.

Q: I'm sick of looking for those damn books Finch needs for her quest. Where are they already?

A: You can find the six books needed for the library at the Bandit Camp (FW1900), Gullykin (FW4002), Pedvale (FW2400), the Carnival outside Nashkel (FW4900) and the Cloakwood Mines (FW1803). Finch has the sixth book already on her person when you meet her in Beregost.

Q: I've found lots of books. How do I know which ones are for Finch's quest?

A: Finch will tell you.

Q: Finch told me we found all the books for the library. What do I do with them?

A: Take them to Nashkel Manor (FW4804) and speak with Trask.

Q: Ruh-roh. I just made those snobs at Nashkel Manor mad, and now Finch has stomped off in a huff! How do I get her back?

A: Wait for Finch SoA? No, really, you can ignore Finch's quest as long as you like without her getting pesky, but if your actions ruin her chances of setting up the library at Nashkel Manor, she'll leave the party FOREVAARRRR. Be more careful what you say or do to the residents next time.

Q: What about Mur'Neth and Indira? Will Finch banter with them?

A: If they are installed, yes, because they are very cool original NPCs.

Q: Is Tales of the Sword Coast required to play with Finch?

A: With version 3 and later, TotSC should not be required to install Finch or complete her quest. If you are playing a version prior to v3, see the Tutu discussion forums on PPG for workarounds.
